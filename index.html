ðŸ”¹ 1. Sole Proprietorship

A form of business owned and controlled by a single person.

Features:
  • Single Ownership
  • Unlimited Liability
  • Control by Owner
  • No Separate Legal Entity
  • Lack of Continuity
  • Quick Decisions
Advantages:
  • Simple and low-cost
  • Full profit to the owner
  • Flexible & personal attention
Limitations:
  • Limited capital
  • Unlimited liability
  • No continuity

ðŸ”¹ 2. Hindu Undivided Family (HUF)

Business by members of a Hindu joint family.

Features:
  • Only Hindus, by birth
  • Karta (eldest male) manages
  • Unlimited liability of Karta
  • Continues after Karta's death
Advantages:
  • Continuity & control
  • Limited liability of members
Limitations:
  • Limited capital
  • Karta may misuse power
  • Only males included

ðŸ”¹ 3. Partnership

A business owned by two or more persons.

Features:
  • Formed by agreement (Deed)
  • Profit/loss sharing
  • Unlimited liability
  • Mutual agency, no legal entity
Types:
  • General, Limited, At Will, Particular
Deed Includes:
  • Partner names, capital, ratio, duties
Advantages:
  • More capital, shared risk
  • Easy to form, combined skills
Limitations:
  • Disputes, no continuity
  • Unlimited liability

ðŸ”¹ 4. Co-operative Society

Voluntary association for mutual benefit.

Features:
  • Open to all, one vote per member
  • Service motive, legal entity
  • Govt. support
Types:
  • Consumer, Producer, Credit, Housing
Advantages:
  • Easy to form, limited liability
  • Helps weaker sections
Limitations:
  • Limited resources, motivation
  • Inefficient management

ðŸ”¹ 5. Joint Stock Company

Owned by shareholders, managed by directors.

Features:
  • Separate legal entity
  • Limited liability, perpetual life
  • Transferable shares
Types:
  • Private: 2-200, can't invite public
  • Public: Min 7, can raise publicly
Key Docs:
  • MOA, AOA, Prospectus
Advantages:
  • Large capital, efficient management
  • Perpetual life, limited liability
Limitations:
  • Complex formation, legal formalities
  • Slower decisions, less secrecy

ðŸ”¹ 6. Public Sector Enterprises

Owned & operated by Govt.

Forms:
  • Departmental (e.g. Railways)
  • Statutory Corp (e.g. LIC)
  • Govt. Company (e.g. BHEL)
Advantages:
  • Serves public interest, job creator
  • Balanced development
Limitations:
  • Political interference
  • Inefficiency, less flexibility
